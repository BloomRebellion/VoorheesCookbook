Sweet Potato Spice Cake

Two-layer cake with cooked frosting

Ingredients

Cake

  • 2 1/2 cups sifted cake flour
  • 3 teaspoons double acting baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 1/2 cups cooking oil
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup hot water
  • 1 1/4 cups grated raw sweet potato
  • 4 egg whites, stiffly beaten

Frosting

  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup butter or margarine
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 1/3 cups flaked coconut
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts

Directions

Makes 2 layers. Preheat oven to 350°F. Sift together the first five ingredients; set aside. Combine the cooking oil and sugar. Beat until well blended.

Add the 4 egg yolks. Beat until light. Add the water and vanilla. Mix well. Add dry ingredients gradually, mixing until smooth after each addition. Add the grated sweet potato and walnuts. Mix well.

Gently fold in the egg whites. Spoon batter into 2 greased 9-inch layer cake pans. Bake at 350°F for 30–35 minutes, or until done. Remove from oven and cool thoroughly on wire racks.

Meanwhile, combine in saucepan the evaporated milk, sugar, egg yolks, margarine, while stirring over medium heat until mixture comes to a boil and thickens. Remove from heat. Beat until well blended and thick enough to spread. Add the coconut and walnuts. Beat until well blended.

Cool slightly. Spread between and on top of cooled cake layers.

Note: This is a moist and very crumby cake.

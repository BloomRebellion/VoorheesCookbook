Fresh Apple Cake

Oven 350°F, 55–60 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups salad oil
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp. each baking powder, baking soda, and salt
  • 2 tsp. vanilla
  • 2 1/2 cups flour
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • 3 cups chopped raw apples

Carmel frosting

  • 1 stick butter (margarine)
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 2 tbsp. evaporated milk
  • 1 cup firmly packed brown sugar
  • Powdered sugar, sifted

Directions

Prepare the raw apples and set aside. Measure the salad oil into a large mixing bowl. Add the sugar and eggs, and beat until creamy. Add the vanilla.

Sift the flour and measure, then sift again with the salt, soda, and baking powder. Add the flour mixture to the creamed mixture a little at a time, beating well after each addition. When all the flour is added, fold in the pecans and apples.

Spread evenly in a 9x12 cake pan lined on the bottom with waxed paper. Bake in a 350°F oven for 55–60 minutes. Turn onto a cake rack and remove the waxed paper. (The batter is very stiff.)

For the frosting: Place the butter, salt, evaporated milk, and brown sugar in a small saucepan. Heat lightly just to melt the butter, mixing until the sugar dissolves. Add enough sifted powdered sugar to reach the right consistency to spread.

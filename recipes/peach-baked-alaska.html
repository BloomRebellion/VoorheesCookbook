Peach Baked Alaska

Oven 400°F, 8–10 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 package (18 1/2 oz.) yellow cake mix
  • 1/2 cup cream sherry
  • 1 can (1 lb. 14 oz.) cling peach halves, drained
  • 1 quart vanilla ice cream
  • 4 egg whites
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup sugar

Directions

Prepare the cake mix as directed on the package. Bake the batter in a greased and floured 9-inch layer cake pan, 2 inches deep. Remove from the pan and cool on a rack. Place the cake on a heat-proof platter or board.

With a sharp knife, cut out the center, leaving a shell about 1 inch thick. Crumble the removed cake and replace it in the cake shell. Sprinkle the crumbs with the sherry. Place the peach halves, rounded side down, on the crumbs.

Scoop the ice cream into balls and put them into the freezer to get hard. When ready to serve, beat the egg whites and salt until stiff. Gradually beat in the sugar, 1 tablespoon at a time, until the mixture is stiff and glossy.

Place the ice cream balls on top of the peaches. Spread the meringue evenly over the cake, peaches, and ice cream, making sure there are no holes.

Bake in a preheated 400°F oven for 8 to 10 minutes, or until the meringue is lightly browned. Cut into wedges and serve at once.

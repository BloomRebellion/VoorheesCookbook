Strawberry Snow Eggs

Ingredients

  • 4 egg whites
  • Dash of salt
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 cups milk
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 pint strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons Cointreau

Directions

Beat the egg whites and salt until stiff. Gradually beat in the 1/2 cup sugar, 1 tablespoon at a time, until stiff and glossy. Pour the milk into a pan so that it is 1 inch deep, and heat over low heat until just hot.

With 2 teaspoons, shape mounds of meringue about the size of an egg. Drop into the hot milk and cook for 1 minute, then turn and cook another minute. Remove from the milk with a slotted spoon and drain on absorbent paper. Repeat using all the meringue mixture. Reserve the milk. Chill the snow eggs.

In a saucepan, combine the egg yolks and 1/4 cup sugar. Gradually stir in the milk in which the snow eggs were cooked. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens and coats a spoon. Do not boil. Stir in the vanilla. Chill.

Combine the strawberries, 1/4 cup sugar, and Cointreau. Chill. When ready to serve, place the snow eggs on a serving platter, spoon the chilled custard over the eggs, and spoon the strawberries over the custard.

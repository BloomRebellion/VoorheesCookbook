Kitchen Basics

A quick, plain-language glossary for anyone new to reading Grandma's recipe cards.

Bake

Cook with dry heat inside the oven.

Beat

Mix quickly and vigorously to add air, usually with a spoon, whisk, or mixer.

Blend

Combine two or more ingredients thoroughly until smooth.

Boil

Heat a liquid until bubbles break steadily on the surface.

Chop

Cut into rough pieces with a knife.

Cream

Beat butter (often with sugar) until soft, light, and fluffy.

Dice

Cut into small, even cubes, about 1/4 inch across.

Fold In

Gently combine a light mixture (like whipped egg whites) into a heavier one without knocking out the air — cut down, sweep across, and lift up.

Sift

Pass a dry ingredient (usually flour) through a fine mesh to remove lumps and lighten it.

Simmer

Cook in liquid that's just below a boil — small, gentle bubbles instead of a rolling boil.

Whip

Beat rapidly to trap air and make a mixture light and fluffy, as with cream or egg whites.

Dash / Pinch

A very small amount, added between the fingers — less than 1/8 teaspoon.
